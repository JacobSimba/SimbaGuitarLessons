What stops people from starting out

Time

"I’m too busy," or "I don’t have time," is one of the most common excuses, especially for people with demanding jobs or family responsibilities. You can start with short, manageable practice sessions, even 10-15 minutes a day, or every other day. Focus on consistency over duration, and you’ll gradually see progress.

Fear of Failure

"I’m not talented enough," or "I’m too old to learn," can discourage people, making them feel they won't succeed. Understand that learning an instrument is a journey, and everyone starts as a beginner. Celebrate small wins along the way to build confidence.

Self-Doubt

"I’ll never be as good as others," or "I don’t want to look silly," stops people from trying, especially in social settings. Focus on your progress rather than comparing yourself to others. Remember, everyone learns at their own pace, and improvement comes with practice.

Procrastination

"I’ll start next week," or "I’ll do it when I have more time," leads to continuous delays. Commit to starting right now, even if it's just picking up the instrument for a few minutes. Setting a regular schedule like practicing at the same time each day can help form a habit.
